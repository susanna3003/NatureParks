North Carolina Regions

From coastal paradises to mountainous vistas, North Carolina's four regions collectively paint a picture of diverse and captivating natural landscapes.

Coast

Along the Atlantic, the Outer Banks steal the spotlight with pristine beaches, historic lighthouses, and unique coastal ecosystems.

Coastal Plains

This region offers a blend of sandy shores and fertile plains, featuring attractions like Cape Hatteras National Seashore and diverse maritime forests.

Piedmont

Known for its rolling hills, the Piedmont region hosts the vibrant city of Charlotte and the historic city of Raleigh, surrounded by a mix of urban amenities and natural charm.

Mountains

The western part of the state showcases the awe-inspiring Blue Ridge Mountains and the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, providing scenic landscapes and outdoor adventures.
